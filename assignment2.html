Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome them

When developing a web page, it is important that developers take into account the accessibility of the website - particularly for individuals with special needs and impairments. Below are some of the issues people with disabilities may face when using web pages:

  1. Hearing: some users may have a reduced ability to hear
  2. Vision: this may include partial or full inability to see or perceive colour contrasts
  3. Photosensitive seizures: conditions such as epilepsy can cause seizures that are triggered by flashing images
  4. Cognitive disabilities: these include conditions such as dementia and dyslexia
  5. Motor skills: other users may have movement issues (i.e. difficulty moving certain parts of the body)

How to overcome these issues

  1. Colour - the choice of colour is really important in overcome issues for people with disabilities. Developers need to make sure they select appropriate colours which will contrast well with the background of the page.
